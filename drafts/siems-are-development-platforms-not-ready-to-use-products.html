SIEMs are Development Platforms, not Ready to Use Products

// under siem

// Tue 02 February 2016

SIEMs are a challenge for everyone. Projects to ensure all log sources are properly synchronizing their timestamps, standardizing the timezones, ensuring device policies are configured for proper log generation alone can take months on any decent-sized organization. And when you finally handle all that and have your shiny new SIEM installed, collecting logs and ready to go... is when you realize that you still have all your work ahead of you.

See, I have believed for the longest time that SIEMs are not products, but are more like a development platform or a database. After all, now is the starting point for you writing the actual SIEM content such as rules, apps, searches, dashboards, what your vendor lingo is. This is basically application development within the SIEM platform, which can and should benefit from all that we've learned from software engineering.
